A day in the field

This is what a trip looks like inside the app — your planner's expert notes, your co-traveler's live sightings, and your personal wish-list all updating together in real time.

Day 3  ·  Masai Mara, Kenya 🌿 Land Realm
5:30am
Wake-up call & packed breakfast
Early start to catch first light on the plains. Predator activity peaks in the two hours after dawn.
6:00am
Morning game drive — Mara River
Head directly to the crossing point. Migration timing is peak — wildebeest are massing on the north bank.
● African Lion — on your list Cheetah Wildebeest
12:30pm
Lunch & rest at camp
Wildlife slows in the midday heat. Review this morning's sightings and update your log before the afternoon drive.
4:00pm
Afternoon drive — Bila Shaka area
Best light of the day. Highest cheetah density in the Mara — and where leopard are often found at dusk.
Lilac-breasted Roller Leopard
7:30pm
Dinner & debrief at camp
Log your sightings. Tomorrow: early departure south toward Amboseli and Kilimanjaro views.
🧭 Your travel planner
"Be at the Mara River by 6:30am. Watch for the herd massing on the north bank — when the lead animals start pacing the water's edge, a crossing is usually 20 minutes away. Don't leave early."

Day 4  ·  Jean-Michel Cousteau Resort, Fiji 🌊 Ocean Realm
6:30am
Pre-dive briefing — Dreamhouse conditions
Current is running well this morning — ideal for pelagics. Visibility 20m+. The dive team recommends descending along the drop-off edge and holding position mid-water to let the sharks come to you.
7:15am
Dive 1 — Dreamhouse
A prime pelagic site on a good current day. Gorgonian sea fans sway along the drop-off edges as barracuda school overhead. Grey reef and white tip sharks patrol the wall while turtles glide through the current below. Recommended for seasoned divers.
● Grey Reef Shark — on your list White Tip Reef Shark Green Sea Turtle Barracuda
10:00am
Surface interval — resort beach
Rest and rehydrate. Review this morning's sightings and log species while the detail is fresh. The house reef is walkable from the beach if you want a gentle snorkel.
1:00pm
Dive 2 — Coral Corner
Shallower afternoon dive into Fiji's famous soft coral gardens. Staghorn and table corals carpeted in purple, orange, and red. Bumphead parrotfish work the reef — you can hear them crunching coral from 10 metres away.
● Bumphead Parrotfish — on your list Napoleonfish Clownfish
4:00pm
Dive 3 — Annie's Bommie
Late afternoon light through the water makes this bommie extraordinary. Mandarin fish emerge at dusk. Sea kraits coil in the coral heads. End the dive as the reef begins its evening shift change.
Mandarin Fish Banded Sea Krait Lionfish
6:30pm
Species log & sunset drinks
Log all three dives before dinner. Three dives, three completely different ecosystems — all within a few kilometres of the resort.
🧭 Your travel planner
"Dreamhouse needs the right current to perform — and today it's perfect. Stay mid-water on the drop-off and let the sharks work around you. Don't chase them. The ones that come closest are the ones you stayed still for."
Added to Day 4 · 5 days ago
👤 Emma · family
"The bumphead parrotfish at Coral Corner — there must have been 40 of them. You could feel the sound through your whole body. I had no idea fish could be that big."
Logged 1:48pm · Dive 2 Coral Corner

Day 2  ·  Mindo Cloud Forest, Ecuador 🐦 Sky Realm
5:00am
Pre-dawn departure from lodge
In position on the trail before first light. The dawn chorus begins 20 minutes before sunrise — this is when the cloud forest comes alive.
5:30am
Dawn birding walk — Mindo valley trail
The fruiting aguacatillo trees at km 3.2 are the quetzal hotspot right now. Move slowly, speak quietly, let the forest settle around you.
● Resplendent Quetzal — on your list Plate-billed Mountain Toucan Cock-of-the-rock
8:30am
Breakfast at lodge & species log
Record this morning's sightings while memory is fresh. Cloud forest birds are notoriously difficult to confirm by description alone.
9:30am
Hummingbird feeders & tanager trail
The lodge feeders attract up to 14 hummingbird species. The trail behind draws tanagers in extraordinary variety — up to 40 species in a morning.
Violet-tailed Sylph Paradise Tanager Booted Racket-tail
4:00pm
Evening walk — antpitta feeding station
The lodge's habituated Giant Antpitta comes to the same spot every evening at 4:15. One of the most reliable encounters in birding.
🧭 Your travel planner
"The quetzal is most active in the 45 minutes after first light. Your guide knows the exact fruiting tree — trust his positioning. If you don't see it by 7am, move to the ridge trail. A second male has been working the area above the waterfall."
